One of the primary critiques of Tom Hooper's 2019 adaptation of "Cats" is its uncanny valley visual effects. The choice to blend human features with those of felines using CGI created an eerie, often unsettling appearance for the characters. This aesthetic choice distracted from the performances and made it challenging for audiences to connect emotionally with the characters, undermining the fantastical charm that the original musical is known for.

Narratively, "Cats" struggled with coherence and engagement. The plot, thin and somewhat episodic in its stage form, translated poorly to film. Without substantial narrative depth or character development, the movie relied heavily on its musical numbers and visual spectacle, which were not enough to maintain viewer interest. This lack of a compelling storyline left many viewers feeling disconnected and confused about the overall direction of the film.

The film's pacing and structure also drew criticism. Scenes often felt disjointed, with musical numbers seeming like isolated set pieces rather than parts of a cohesive whole. This disjointedness was exacerbated by erratic editing that failed to smooth the transitions between scenes, contributing to a choppy and sometimes disorienting viewing experience.

Musically, while "Cats" boasts an iconic score by Andrew Lloyd Webber, the film's renditions of these beloved songs were met with mixed reactions. The new arrangements and interpretations, including the controversial addition of the song "Beautiful Ghosts," did not always resonate with audiences, especially purists of the original musical. Moreover, the sound mixing was often criticized for not effectively balancing the music with vocals and ambient sounds, which detracted from the film's auditory appeal.

The ensemble cast of "Cats," despite being studded with stars, was another point of contention. While there were standout performances, the overwhelming use of CGI and makeup often masked the actors' abilities to convey emotion and nuance through their performances. This not only wasted the talent of an impressive cast but also left many characters feeling flat and unmemorable.

Lastly, the critical and public reception of "Cats" highlighted a disconnect between filmmakers' ambitions and audience expectations. The decision to prioritize visual effects over narrative and character development was a gamble that did not pay off. This misalignment not only resulted in a bewildering cinematic experience but also left "Cats" as a cautionary tale about the risks of adapting beloved stage works without sufficient consideration for the medium's demands.
