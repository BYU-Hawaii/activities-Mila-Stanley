Koreatown (K-town), Chinatown, and Little Tokyo in Los Angeles each offer unique cultural experiences and a variety of activities for visitors.

Koreatown

Koreatown is a vibrant neighborhood known for its bustling nightlife, delicious cuisine, and rich cultural offerings. You can start your visit with a meal at one of the many Korean BBQ restaurants or enjoy a relaxing day at a Korean spa. Explore the historic Wiltern Theatre for live performances or indulge in some karaoke at one of the many karaoke bars. The neighborhood is also home to a variety of markets and shops selling Korean goods.

Chinatown

Chinatown is a colorful district that offers a mix of traditional and contemporary attractions. Wander through the Central Plaza to admire the Chinese-style architecture and sculptures, and visit the Thien Hau Temple for a touch of spirituality. The district is filled with specialty shops where you can find everything from herbal medicine to unique trinkets. Don't miss the chance to sample authentic Chinese cuisine at the many restaurants and dim sum spots. For a bit of history, check out the Chinese American Museum.

Little Tokyo

Little Tokyo is a historic and cultural hub for Japanese Americans in Los Angeles. Start your visit at the Japanese American National Museum to learn about the history and contributions of Japanese Americans. Stroll through the Japanese Village Plaza for shopping, dining, and unique cultural experiences, such as tea ceremonies. Enjoy traditional and contemporary Japanese cuisine at the many restaurants, from sushi bars to ramen shops. Little Tokyo also hosts a variety of festivals and cultural events throughout the year, adding to its vibrant atmosphere.
